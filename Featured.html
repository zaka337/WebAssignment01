Fiddle music is a traditional style of folk music that originated in Europe and has since spread to various parts of the world. It is characterized by lively and energetic melodies, often played on the violin, but can also be played on other instruments such as the accordion or the bagpipes. Fiddle music is often associated with social gatherings, such as weddings and ceilidhs, where it is used to get people dancing and celebrating.

The style of fiddle music can vary greatly depending on the region, with different countries and cultures having their own unique traditions and techniques. For example, Irish fiddle music is known for its fast-paced and intricate melodies, while Scottish fiddle music is often more subdued and haunting. Overall, fiddle music is a lively and infectious style of music that is sure to get your feet tapping.
